Think Twice


Think Twice: A Mystery That Keeps You Guessing Harlan Coben's "Think Twice" is a thrilling ride that will keep you on the edge of your seat. It follows Myron Bolitar, a sports agent with a knack for finding trouble. This time, trouble finds him when the FBI shows up suspecting his client of a double murder! The story gets twisty and turny fast. There's a clever killer on the loose, leaving confusing clues and framing innocent people. Myron, with the help of his sharp wit and loyal friend Win, dives into the investigation. The book is full of suspense, with unexpected revelations around every corner. You'll find yourself questioning everything you think you know and suspecting everyone! Coben does a great job of building tension and keeping you guessing until the very end. Here's what makes "Think Twice" a good read: Fast-paced and exciting: The story moves quickly, with new developments and clues popping up all the time. Compelling characters: You'll root for Myron, a likable and relatable protagonist. Mind-bending plot: The twists and turns will keep you surprised and guessing who the real villain is. Some things to keep in mind: This is the 12th book in the Myron Bolitar series. While you can still enjoy it without reading the others, there might be references to past events. The book deals with some serious themes, like murder and violence. Overall, "Think Twice" is a great choice for anyone who enjoys a good mystery with a healthy dose of suspense.
